# Theme & Narrative — Notes

Project: 2026-camping-minecraft
Created: 2026-04-18

## Setting
- Lake Fairfax park campsite
- Real outdoor campsite with rocks, streams, trail loop, trees, bathrooms
- Natural "biomes": trees = forest, rocks = mountains, stream = river/ocean, bathroom = abandoned mine

## Audience & Tone
- Kids ages 8–13, up to 6 kids
- Friends and family, not commercial
- Adventurous, silly, fun — not scary
- ~45 minutes target

## Story
An Ender Dragon egg has been detected somewhere near the campsite. The kids are Minecraft explorers sent to find it. They need to explore biomes, collect blocks, learn recipes, and craft their way to the dig site.

## Core Mechanic
- 3D-printed blocks with RFID tags hidden across the campsite in biome-appropriate spots
- One RFID crafting table as the tech centerpiece (Arduino/Pi powered, battery)
- Blocks are scattered from the start — exploration is open-world
- Recipes are gated — each successful craft reveals the next recipe card
- **1–2 iPads/phones with the fog-of-war map app** — kids carry these to explore and find blocks. The map reveals terrain as they walk, Minecraft-style. Block hiding spots are pre-marked as item markers on the map.
- **MCompass** — a real Minecraft compass that always points toward the tent (crafting table home base). Kids carry it while exploring so they can always find their way back. Set spawn point to the tent GPS coordinates.
- Everything except the crafting table and digital map is analog
- Game master acts as "NPC" to verify tool-gated steps (e.g., pickaxe to enter the mine)

## The 5 Main Crafts

1. **Wooden Pickaxe** (3 wood planks + 2 sticks)
   - Starting recipe, given to kids at the beginning
   - Wood/stick blocks hidden near trees (forest biome)
   - Craft gives them a wooden pickaxe prop
   - Unlocks the "Abandoned Mine" (bathroom) — sign on door: "⛏ Abandoned Mine — Wooden Pickaxe Required"
   - Inside the mine: iron ingots, string, diamond blocks hidden around the bathroom
   - Reveals Recipe Card #2

2. **Fishing Rod** (3 sticks + 2 string + 1 iron)
   - String and iron from the mine, sticks from forest biome
   - Craft gives them a magnetic fishing rod (stick + string + magnet)
   - Fish gold ingot blocks out of the stream (waterproof container with metal ring/washer, magnet snags it)
   - Reveals Recipe Card #3

3. **Gold Sword** (2 gold ingots + 1 stick)
   - Gold from fishing, sticks from forest
   - Craft gives them a foam gold sword
   - Clue sends them to the creeper (stacked cardboard boxes, painted green, pre-scored to fall apart)
   - Kids smash the creeper — map ingredients (paper blocks, compass block) spill out
   - Reveals Recipe Card #4

4. **Map** (8 paper + 1 compass)
   - Paper and compass from the creeper
   - Craft unlocks the fog-of-war map on the iPad/phone — game master hands over the device with the map app loaded
   - The digital map shows the campsite in Minecraft style with fog covering unexplored areas
   - Block hiding spots are marked on the map as item icons (only visible once the fog is cleared by walking near them)
   - The dig site is marked on the map as a special marker — kids have to explore to reveal it
   - The map also shows the player's real-time GPS position
   - Optionally: a second map device can be given so kids can split up
   - Reveals Recipe Card #5

5. **Diamond Shovel** (2 diamonds + 1 stick)
   - Diamonds from the mine, sticks from forest
   - Craft gives them a real shovel painted diamond blue/teal
   - Go dig at the X on the map

## Finale
- Pre-buried waterproof container at the dig site (6–8 inches deep)
- Inside: 3D-printed Ender Dragon egg (painted, keepsake) + treats/prizes for everyone
- Kids dig it up with the diamond shovel

## Bonus Discovery Recipes (no recipe card — kids experiment and find these)
- **TNT** (4 sand + 5 gunpowder) → pop rocks candy
- **Cookie** (2 wheat + 1 cocoa bean) → actual cookies
- Bonus ingredients scattered in biomes alongside main blocks
- Rewards are small treats — not plot-critical

## Extras
- Minecraft fox stuffed animal hidden somewhere, holding a hint to a hard-to-find block
- Older kids will appreciate the gold sword being famously terrible in Minecraft
- Diamond ore vs diamond blocks distinction is a detail Minecraft kids will love

## Adventure Arc
Mine → Fish → Fight → Navigate (with digital map + compass) → Dig

## Tools the Kids Carry
- **MCompass** — always points back to the tent/crafting table. Given at the start or after the first craft. Helps kids navigate back from distant biomes.
- **Fog Map (1–2 devices)** — unlocked by crafting the Map (recipe #4). Shows Minecraft-style terrain with fog of war. Block locations and the dig site appear as markers when the fog clears. Real-time GPS tracking.
